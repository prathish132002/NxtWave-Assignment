The rapid rise of remote work has sparked a massive debate about the future of the office. While some companies are pushing for a full "return-to-office" (RTO) mandate, many employees are advocating for a hybrid or fully remote model. Proponents of remote work point to increased productivity and a better work-life balance, while skeptics worry about the loss of spontaneous collaboration and company culture. Data from the last year suggests that companies offering flexibility have higher retention rates, but there are also concerns about "proximity bias" where onsite workers get promoted faster. Ultimately, the "best" model probably depends on the industry and the specific goals of the team.
